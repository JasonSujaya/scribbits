# Scribbits Suno Soundtrack

## The sound of Scribbits

Scribbits should sound handmade, bright, and slightly unruly: a tiny indie-pop
band playing with things found on a drawing desk. The recurring palette is
ukulele or muted nylon guitar, glockenspiel, toy piano, warm bass, brush drums,
handclaps, playful whistles, pencil taps, paper flicks, and soft page swishes.

Every track should reuse a simple rising four-note **Scribbits motif**. Keep the
music melodic but leave the center of the mix open so hit, shield, knockout,
and UI cues remain readable. Only the main theme uses lyrics.

Use this exclusion text for every Suno generation:

> epic cinematic orchestra, trailer booms, aggressive EDM, trap hi-hats, heavy
> metal guitars, corporate advertising music, saccharine preschool music,
> comedy sound effects, muddy mix, long ambient intro, spoken dialogue

small musical accents; one recognizable battle identity is stronger and much
lighter to ship.

## Short musical cues

These should be cut from the chosen tracks so the whole game sounds related:

- **Birth:** 2.5 seconds; the main motif rises, pauses, then lands with a clap
  and glockenspiel sparkle.
- **Victory:** 3 seconds; fast full motif with a warm final chord.
- **Defeat:** 2 seconds; first three motif notes descend into one soft paper tap,
  never a sad trombone.
- **Sudden Scribble:** 1 second; accelerating pencil roll into the battle motif's
  highest note.
- **Capsule reveal:** 2 seconds; toy-piano question answered by a glockenspiel
  chord.
- **Legacy earned:** 4 seconds; the slow Gallery motif gains one bright final
  note.

Battle hit, shield, critical, bell, and knockout sounds remain separate from the
music. They communicate gameplay and must always cut through the mix.

## Selection rule

Generate four versions of **Drawn Alive**, **Ready, Set, Scribble!**, and
**Morning on the Page** first. Choose the take whose four-note motif is easiest
to remember after one listen. Use that winning motif as the reference audio for
all remaining generations; do not choose each song independently.
